Expert systems

Created Tuesday 26 May 2015
(Liebowitz, 1992) proposes an expert systems course schedule formed by the following topics:

Problem selection

Selecting the "right problem" following these tips:

  • Is good that you already familiar with the domain.
  • A problem that is causing a large number of people, a fair amount of grief.
  • Problem initially too small and probably scope that down further.
  • Get an expert who is willing to work with you.
  • Have a backup expert.
  • Define with the expert the expected accuracy.
  • Don't overpromise.
  • Rapid prototyping. "build a little, test a little"

Knowledge Acquisition

  • One of the most difficult tasks to accomplish.
  • make sure that you are familiar/comfortable with the domain.
  • the first session with the expert should be an introductory lecture on the task at hand (by the expert). You can check your limits, your understanding and the expert quality.
  • have a systematic approach to acquiring knowledge.
  • incorporate the input and feedback from the expert into the expert system. Motivate and involve the expert.
  • Pick up manuals and documentation on the subject matter. More background for your questions.
  • Tape as possible the knowledge acquisition sessions.

Knowledge Representation

  • Representing the knowledge in the knowledge base as rules, frames, scripts, semantic networks, or some hybrid.
  • Representation method which most closely resembles the way the expert is thinking and expressing his/her knowledge: if....then (rule). Or if it is more descriptive and context-dependent (frames and semantic networks)
  • Consider whether uncertainty should play a part in your system. Define your tolerance.
  • Consider the expert reasons in a data-driven manner or a goal-directed manner

Knowledge Encoding

  • Determine the requirements of the task, instead of force-fitting a shell to a task
  • Knowledge base in a modular format for ease of updating
  • Appropriate and adequate user interface
  • Use an incremental, iterative approach for prototype. "build a little, test a little"

Knowledge Testing and Evaluation

  • //Verification and Validation:// check for consistency of the knowledge/logic have a representative set of test cases.
  • //Evaluate:// expose prototype to human factors (users).

Implementation and maintenance

  • After developing, train the users.
  • Make documentation.
  • Assign and instruct a maintenance person/group.
