Definition of thermoeconomics / exergoeconomics

  • (El-Sayed, 2003) stated that all models in thermoeconomic analysis are formulated simply to answer the following question:
"is there a system concept that can have higher energy efficiency and lower unit production cost than an existing one under the same boundary conditions?"
  • Following the statement presented in (El-Sayed, 2003), following cases can be found:
    1. when the system presents higher efficiency and lower cost it is accepted.
    2. when the system presents lower efficiency and higher cost it is rejected.
    3. when the system presents higher efficiency and higher cost, further research and devlopment is required.
    4. when the system presents lower efficiency and lower cost, fuel scarcity and environmental concerns should be considered.
  • (Sciubba, 2005) present the exergoeconomics as a branch of applied thermodynamics that deals with second-law based process synthesis and optimization methods.
  • According (Sciubba, 2005), thermoeconomics consider a monetary product cost optimized in terms of exergy considerations. While exergoeconomics transform monetary expenses into exergy fluxes and operate only on exergy fluxes.
  • Some authors (like treat exergoeconomics and thermoeconomics as synonyms. However, (Sciubba, 2005) considers thermoeconomics as a special case or exergoeconomics.
  • (Sciubba, 2005) adopted the Tsatsaronis definition of thermoeconomics: an exergy-aided cost minimization. Which combines engineering economics and exergy analysis to find an 'optimal' process structure and design.


Objective of thermoeconomics

  • According to (El-Sayed, 2003), thermoeconomics has been used mainly for following targets:
    • pinpoint and quantify energy ineficiencies.
    • provision of insight to improvement.
    • automation of certain aspects of the search of improvement.

Logic principles of thermoeconomics

  • Requirement of material for making an energy conversion device (surface area) and the requirement of the driving energy to perform its task (fuel consumption), are both expenses often in strong confict (El-Sayed, 2003) . The first is related directly to the capital cost and the second is related directly to the fuel resource cost. Total cost is the sum of both costs rated per unit time.

History of thermoeconomics

  • First thermoeconomics models were developed for seawater desalinisation units during the decade of 60's by Tribus and evans (El-Sayed, 2003),
  • First works were published in Europe and then in US (Sciubba, 2005)
  • Professor Tribus coined the word "Thermoeconomics" (El-Sayed, 2003).
  • Concept of thermoeconomics was well developed in academic circles, but it never really made an impact on process engineers, industrial managers and energy planners until the late 1980's (Sciubba, 2005).
  • Valero, between 1986 and 1989 derived a general and completely formalized costing theory to calculate the exergetic cost of a product from the exergetic input into the proccess and the structure of the production process (Sciubba, 2005).

How thermoeconomics address system complexity

  • (El-Sayed, 2003) mentions four techniques that have been used for managing system complexity. They are all system-structure oriented (contrasting equation-solution oriented of conventional optimization)
    1. system decomposition consistent with mathematics of optimization, by which internal prices are computed not asumed. Most work with this aproach comes under the term "thermoeconomics".
    2. Assume internal pricing methodologies for system decomposition. Most of the work with this approach comes under the term "exergoeconomics".
    3. Construction of a composite heat exchange profile for identifiying where to add heat, reject heat, to produce work or supply work properly. All the work with this method comes under the term "pinch technology"
    4. Let the computer automate the analysis by supplying it with a arge database of devices and their characteristics . All the work with this method comes under the term "expert systems" or "artificial intelligence".
  • According to (El-Sayed, 2003), the fourth technique treats a problem as a selection from a large resevoir of solutions by an artificial intelligence code - It can be a misinterpretation. This approach is a fast pragmatic approach. it has less impact on innovative energy conversion devices
